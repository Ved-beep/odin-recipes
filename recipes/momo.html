How to Make Nepali Momo

Nepali Momo is a delicious and popular street food in Nepal. These steamed dumplings are filled with a flavorful mixture of vegetables or meat, wrapped in soft dough, and steamed to perfection. It's a must-try dish, and here's how you can make it at home!

Momo Ingredients

The beauty of momo is in its versatility. You can easily customize the filling based on your preference. Here's the basic ingredient list for making momo:

Meat or Vegetables :
Traditionally, momo is made with ground chicken, buffalo, or pork. You can also make vegetarian momo with finely chopped vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and mushrooms.
Onion and Garlic :
Finely chopped onion and garlic give the filling a savory kick.
Ginger :
Fresh ginger is an essential ingredient for adding flavor to the filling.
Soy Sauce :
Soy sauce is used to enhance the flavor of the filling, especially in non-vegetarian momo.
Spices and Seasonings :
Ground cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder are the core spices in momo filling.
All-purpose flour :
This forms the dough for the momo wrapper.
Oil :
You'll need a little oil to grease the momo steamer and for the dough.

How to Make Nepali Momo Step-By-Step

Here's a quick overview of how to make delicious Nepali momo at home:

  1. Prepare the dough.
  2. Prepare the filling.
  3. Stuff the momo wrappers with the filling.
  4. Steam the momo in a steamer for about 15-20 minutes.
  5. Serve with spicy momo chutney.

How to Prepare Momo Dough

The dough for momo is simple and soft. Itâ€™s made with all-purpose flour, water, and a pinch of salt. You need to knead the dough until it is smooth and elastic. Let it rest for about 30 minutes before you start making the wrappers.

How to Make the Momo Filling

To prepare the filling, combine your choice of meat or vegetables with finely chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and the necessary spices. Add soy sauce for a savory flavor. Mix everything well until the ingredients are well incorporated.

How to Stuff and Shape the Momo

Roll out small portions of dough into thin circles. Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center. Carefully pinch the edges of the dough together, pleating as you go to form a tight, sealed dumpling. You can shape them into a traditional round or half-moon shape.

How Long to Steam Momo

Once the momo are shaped, place them in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper to avoid sticking. Steam for about 15-20 minutes or until the dough turns translucent and the filling is cooked through. The momo will be soft and juicy once ready!

How to Serve Nepali Momo

Serve your freshly steamed momo with spicy momo chutney made from tomatoes, green chilies, garlic, and a little oil. Enjoy the soft, flavorful momo with a hot cup of Nepali tea or as a snack on its own!

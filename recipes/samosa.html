Delicious Aloo Samosa

How to Make Aloo Samosa

Aloo Samosa is a classic snack enjoyed in many parts of the world, especially in South Asia. These golden, crispy pastries are filled with a flavorful spiced potato filling, perfect for any occasion. Here's how you can make them at home!

Aloo Samosa Ingredients

The ingredients for making Aloo Samosa are simple but packed with flavor. Below is a list of the essential ingredients for both the filling and the dough:

Potatoes :
The base of the filling is boiled and mashed potatoes. They provide the perfect texture for the samosa filling.
Onions :
Finely chopped onions give the filling a slight crunch and enhance the flavor.
Ginger and Garlic :
Freshly minced ginger and garlic add a fragrant, savory taste to the filling.
Spices and Seasonings :
Ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and red chili powder are used to flavor the filling. You can also add amchur (dry mango powder) for a tangy kick.
Green Chilies :
Chopped green chilies give the samosa a spicy heat, but feel free to adjust based on your spice tolerance.
Coriander Leaves :
Fresh chopped cilantro (coriander leaves) adds brightness and freshness to the filling.
Wheat Flour :
Used to make the dough for the samosa wrapper. The dough is simple, made with flour, water, and a pinch of salt.
Oil :
You’ll need oil for frying the samosas to get that crispy golden texture.

How to Make Aloo Samosa Step-By-Step

Making Aloo Samosas is a multi-step process, but the results are totally worth it. Here's a brief overview of the steps:

  1. Make the dough for the samosa wrappers.
  2. Prepare the spiced potato filling.
  3. Shape the samosas and stuff them with the filling.
  4. Heat oil and fry the samosas until crispy and golden.
  5. Serve with chutney and enjoy!

How to Make the Samosa Dough

To make the dough, combine wheat flour, a pinch of salt, and a little oil in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a smooth and firm dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20 minutes before rolling it out.

How to Prepare the Aloo Filling

For the filling, boil potatoes until soft. Once cooled, peel and mash them. In a pan, heat some oil and sauté onions, ginger, garlic, and green chilies until golden. Then, add your spices (cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder) and cook briefly. Finally, mix in the mashed potatoes, add chopped coriander leaves, and adjust salt to taste. Let the mixture cool before using it as the filling.

How to Shape the Samosas

Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a circle. Cut the circle in half, forming two semi-circles. Shape each semi-circle into a cone by folding it in half and sealing the edges. Stuff the cone with the potato filling, then pinch the open edges together to seal the samosa, ensuring it's tightly closed to avoid any filling leakage during frying.

How Long to Fry the Samosas

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, gently slide the samosas into the oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them in batches for about 4-5 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Be sure to turn them occasionally for even cooking.

How to Serve Aloo Samosa

Serve your hot, crispy Aloo Samosas with tangy tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or a spicy tomato dip. Enjoy these delicious treats with a hot cup of chai or as a snack any time of the day!
